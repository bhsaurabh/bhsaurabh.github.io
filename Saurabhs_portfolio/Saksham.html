Project Saksham

 

India’s 1st Gesture imitating humanoid robot

Project Saksham involves the development of a one of its kind humanoid robot, that is capable of mimicking gestures by seeing them!

Simulation

Before implementation, every program is tested with simulation.

The simulation models the impact of every force accurately and can display the effects of various forces on the different parts of the robot.

The robot’s various parts are designed with precision using AutoDesk Inventor. These parts are then imported to Microsoft Robotics Developer Studio to perform simulations.


Shown alongside is the WireFrame View of the right arm.

The simulation allows for frame assignment and modeling the various forces that can cause changes in the robot’s orientations.

Human Mimicry

Saksham is capable of vision guided gesture mimicry. It uses the Kinect sensor (made by Microsoft for the XBox Consoles).

The gesture recognition is programmed in C# and is integrated with MRDS on Visual Studio.
